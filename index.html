The 6.3% MFV is applied to the base job amount (what the client pays). But drivers receive a percentage of the base as their pay — so the fuel component is a higher proportion of the courier payment. The most common effective ratio is ~11%, which means the typical driver commission rate is roughly 55–60%.

Verdict

The MFV matrix is fit for purpose for 97% of driver-days.


For the core Auckland Hiace fleet doing urban round-town work (up to ~500km/day), the fuel adjustment more than covers the additional fuel cost versus the $1.86 base — for both petrol and diesel vehicles.


The only gap is on high-km days (500km+), representing just 3% of driver-days. These appear to be regional or linehaul runs where the driver covers significant distance but earns comparatively less revenue per km. In these cases, the petrol shortfall averages ~$18/day.


Options for the 500km+ gap:

  • Accept as-is (only 3% of days, small population)
  • Introduce a vehicle-size or run-type override for regional/linehaul
  • Apply a km-based floor to the MFV calculation for long-distance work
